## Regional Performance 2
- West is the strongest region: $725,458 in sales and $108,419 profit (~14.9% margin) — highest of all regions.
- Central region ranks 3rd in sales ($501,240) but has the weakest profit margin (~7.9%, only $39,706 profit) — sales volume isn't translating to profit as efficiently as in West/East.
- South is the smallest region by sales ($391,722) but maintains a healthy ~11.9% margin.

## Category Performance 3 
- Technology is the most profitable category: $836,154 sales, $145,456 profit (17.4% margin).
- Office Supplies performs nearly as well: $719,047 sales, $122,491 profit (17.0% margin).
- Furniture is the standout problem: despite generating the 2nd-highest sales ($741,999), 
  it returns only $18,451 profit — a margin of just 2.49%. High sales volume is not 
  translating into profit, suggesting heavy discounting or low-margin product mix within Furniture.

  ## Loss-Making Sub-Categories 6 
- Tables is the biggest problem area: -$17,725.59 in total profit — the largest loss of any sub-category.
- Bookcases also operates at a loss: -$3,472.56.
- Supplies (Office Supplies category) shows a smaller loss: -$1,188.99.
- Tables and Bookcases are both Furniture sub-categories — this explains why Furniture has 
  high sales ($741,999) but a near-zero profit margin (2.49%). These two sub-categories alone 
  account for over $21K in losses, dragging down the entire category's profitability.

  ## Top Products by Sales 9 
- Canon imageCLASS 2200 Advanced Copier is the #1 product by sales ($61,599), 
  nearly 2.3x the next highest product.
- 7 of the top 10 best-selling products are Technology items (copiers, printers, 
  binding machines) — reinforcing Technology as the strongest category.
- Only one Furniture product (HON Task Chairs, $21,870) appears in the top 10, 
  and notably it is NOT one of the loss-making sub-categories (Tables/Bookcases).

  ## Monthly Sales Trends (2014–2017) 13
- Sales follow a clear seasonal pattern: Q3 (especially September) and Q4 
  (November–December) are consistently the strongest months.
- September 2014 was the single highest month at $81,777 in sales.
- January and February are consistently the weakest months — post-holiday 
  slowdown is visible every year.
- July 2014 showed negative profit (-$841) despite $33,946 in sales, 
  suggesting heavy discounting that month.
